Cookie Policy

Last updated: 23 February 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how FAST FR8 Logistics Ltd ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website www.fastfr8.co.uk (the "Site").

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, remember your preferences, and provide information to the site owners. Cookies may be "persistent" (remain on your device until deleted) or "session" (deleted when you close your browser).

Types of cookies we use

We use the following categories of cookies on our Site:

1. Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the Site to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, and accessibility. You cannot opt out of these cookies, but you can set your browser to block them – however, this may affect the Site's performance.

2. Performance / Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with the Site by collecting anonymous information such as pages visited, time spent, and error messages. We use this data to improve the Site. The information collected is aggregated and does not directly identify you. We may use third‑party services such as Google Analytics for this purpose.

3. Functional Cookies

Functional cookies allow the Site to remember choices you make (such as your language or region) and provide enhanced, more personal features. They may also be used to remember changes you have made to text size, fonts, and other parts of web pages you can customise.

4. Targeting / Marketing Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver adverts more relevant to you and your interests. They may also limit the number of times you see an advertisement and help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns. They are usually placed by advertising networks with our permission. (We currently do not use marketing cookies, but this may change; we will update this policy accordingly.)

Third‑Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we may also use various third‑party cookies to report usage statistics and improve the Site. These third parties may include, for example, Google Analytics. Their use of cookies is subject to their own privacy policies.

Managing Cookies

When you first visit our Site, you will see a cookie banner that allows you to accept or reject non‑essential cookies.

Most browsers also allow you to refuse or delete cookies. The method for doing so varies from browser to browser; please consult your browser's help menu for instructions.

Please note that if you disable cookies, some parts of the Site may not function properly.

Your Rights (UK GDPR / PECR)

Under UK data protection law, you have the right to be informed about the cookies we use and to give or withhold consent for non‑essential cookies. We obtain your consent via the cookie banner before setting any non‑essential cookies.

Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, legislation, or our practices. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date. We encourage you to review this policy regularly.
